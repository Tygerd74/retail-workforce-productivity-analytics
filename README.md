This study evaluates how workforce scheduling decisions influence operational throughput and labor productivity using transaction-level retail data encompassing 996,569 transactions across 126 employees.Fixed-effects regression models were developed to isolate the effects of hour-of-day and day-of-week scheduling consistency while controlling for transaction characteristics, labor demand, and temporal factors. Results demonstrate that increased scheduling consistency is positively associated with checkout productivity, although diminishing returns emerge when multiple dimensions of consistency are simultaneously optimized. Scenario analysis suggests that modest improvements in schedule consistency could generate substantial labor savings, highlighting workforce scheduling as a viable lever for operational efficiency improvement in retail environments.
