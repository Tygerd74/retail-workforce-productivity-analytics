Results indicated that a 10-percentage-point improvement in hour-of-day scheduling consistency was associated with an estimated 0.27% productivity increase, while a similar improvement in day-of-week consistency produced an estimated 0.30% increase. Simultaneously improving both measures by 10 percentage points yielded an estimated 0.50% productivity gain, equivalent to approximately 16.8 labor hours saved per employee annually and over 2,100 labor hours saved across the workforce. Larger consistency improvements of 15 percentage points produced estimated gains exceeding 3,000 annual labor hours, demonstrating the potential operational impact of schedule optimization initiatives at scale.
